Top US College Acceptance Rates, 2007–2030

Overall undergraduate admit rates, by entering class · Ivies + Stanford, MIT, UChicago
Plateauing at the floor. The big collapse was 2020 → 2022: COVID + test-optional doubled application volumes and slammed admit rates from ~6–9% to ~3–5%. The last four cycles have bounced sideways inside that band. Where Class-of-2030 numbers were released this April, they ticked down a notch more (Columbia 4.94 → 4.23, Yale 4.60 → 4.24, Brown 5.65 → 5.35) — so the direction is still down, but the slope is mostly flat.
Notes: Acceptance rates are by graduating class year (e.g. "Class of 2025" was admitted in spring 2021). NYP = "not yet published" — Harvard, Princeton, Penn, Dartmouth, and Cornell withheld official Class-of-2029 and Class-of-2030 numbers. The Class-of-2030 points for those schools are aggregator estimates; Columbia, Yale, and Brown 2030 are official. Stanford has not released regular-decision data since Class of 2024. Sources: Ivy Coach, Top Tier Admissions, Oriel Admissions, MIT Admissions.
