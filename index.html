Happy Father's Day

I went back through everything I've been building this year and pulled out the pieces I thought you'd actually enjoy — some ideas you can poke at, some things to look at, a couple of things to make. It all runs right in your browser. No login, no account, nothing to install — just click and go. Best on a laptop or computer (a few of these want a real keyboard and a bigger screen); they'll work on your phone too, just smaller.

A heads-up: these are homemade and a little experimental, so one might act up here or there. If something looks stuck, just refresh the page — nothing can break.

Ideas to explore

poke at them and see how they work
📐Scale

How big is that, really?

Type any measurement — 4,000 acres, 85 decibels, 50 liters, 200°C — and see what it actually means: drawn to scale on a real map, or placed on a scale of things you already know.
🧲Physics

Why magnets can't float

A live slice through a magnetic field. Drop a magnet in and watch it always find a way to escape — there's a 200-year-old theorem that says it must, and here you can feel it.
📉Data

The college-admissions collapse

How hard it's become to get into the top US schools, 2007 → 2030 — the real acceptance rates, honestly annotated, with the COVID-era cliff right there in the data.
🚗3D Sim

Self-driving scenarios

A 3D sandbox of autonomous-vehicle ideas — platoons, city blocks, morning commutes — eleven scenes, each with sliders to reshape it and see what changes.

Things to look at

put one on the big screen
🌌Generative

Living Screensaver

29 ever-moving scenes — plasma fields, auroras, swarms that quietly spell out the time. Pure visual calm; a different one every day.
🎨Generative

The Rectangle Artists

An endless gallery of abstract paintings, generated live in the styles of Mondrian, Rothko, Albers and more. Refresh for a brand-new museum.
🔤WebGL

Letters made of glass

Type, and the letters become 3D objects floating in space — everything else is glass. Allow the camera and tilt your head; the whole scene moves with you.
🏠3D Capture

A room, in 3D

A real room, shot on a 360° camera and rebuilt as a cloud of a few hundred thousand glowing points you can fly straight through. (Give it a few seconds to load.)

🧩3D Toy

Cycletime

A 3D tinker-toy. Drag, build and chain little machines together and watch the physics play out — for the part of you that likes to take things apart.

Waveform

Your voice, turned into a live ribbon of light. Talk, hum or sing — and if you hit record, the clip saves straight to your own device. Nothing's uploaded.
